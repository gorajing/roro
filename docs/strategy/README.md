# roro — strategy & research

The reasoning behind what roro is and where it's going. Four documents, read in order:

1. [**00 · What roro actually does**](00-what-roro-does.md) — the whole thing collapsed to the concrete product. Start here.
2. [**01 · The capability vision**](01-vision.md) — the best-possible roro: north star, the defining wedge (the See-and-Point body), the capability architecture, the moat, and what roro won't do. Includes the four adversarial critic teardowns.
3. [**02 · Digital-pet research**](02-digital-pet-research.md) — 30 years of digital pets (Tamagotchi → Aibo → Finch → AI companions): what makes people love them, the anti-patterns, and what roro should steal without breaking its never-needy invariant. "Stakes without threat."
4. [**03 · Casual users**](03-casual-users.md) — extending roro beyond the codebase: the verifiability firewall, the segments ranked by real fit, and why the pointing *body* reaches everyone but the memory *spine* should only reach verifiable repeat-users.

**The build ahead** (see `docs/plans/`): the **paw-on-the-pixel** wedge — drop the cat on a thing, it points back at the exact pixel with a plain answer.

These are north-star thinking artifacts, not specs. roro's locked invariants remain the source of truth: local-first/$0, point-don't-act, present≠watching, restraint/never-needy, fail-loud, owner-scoped memory, mouth-not-brain voice, no telemetry.
