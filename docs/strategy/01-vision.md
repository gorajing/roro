# roro — The Definitive Capability Vision

## 1. North Star

**roro is a pixel-cat that lives on your glass, points at the exact thing you mean, and remembers how you work — entirely on your machine, entirely yours.**

The coding-companion↔ambient-pet tension is not a compromise to manage; it is resolved by a single mechanic. roro's **body is the interface**. You pick the cat up and drop it on a line, a diff hunk, a stack frame, a UI element, and say "this — what's wrong here." Gesture supplies the *referent*; your words supply only the *intent*. roro answers the same way it is asked: it trots across a click-through overlay and rests a paw on the exact pixel that matters. The pet's form (present, draggable, on your screen) *is* the coding tool's function (resolving reference, the costliest friction in every assistant). They were never two products. Pointing at a bug and pointing at a settings toggle are the same gesture.

The memory is what the body earns. Every time you point, roro sees a sliver of how you actually work — and over weeks it becomes the one thing a cloud assistant structurally cannot be: the private, continuous thread of *your* working life, held by something that runs with the network unplugged. The cat is the wedge and the growth loop. The memory is the moat that thickens behind it.

roro concedes that a frontier model will out-reason it on any single prompt. It doesn't compete there. It competes on the two things a cloud window can never have: **a body on your glass, and a memory you can feed the truth to.**

## 2. The Defining Capability

**The See-and-Point Body — deictic addressing as a two-way native grammar.**

*What:* Drop the cat on the exact thing, speak intent; roro grounds the answer back onto the same spot with a paw, an arrow, or a short sequence of points — the answer lands *on* the code, never in a sidebar you have to re-anchor. *Why 10x:* it deletes reference-description ("the function above, no, the other one") — the friction tax on every assistant conversation — and it is structurally impossible for a window, panel, or browser tab, because they have no body on your screen. It works in the first five minutes with zero accumulated memory, it is the one inherently shareable artifact a local tool can produce (a GIF of a cat walking to the failing line sells itself), and every point feeds the perception stream the memory later eats.

**Why the body, not the memory.** The earlier draft crowned "Recall of Intent." That was wrong on three counts, and the critiques are right. (a) Cloud is *not* amnesiac in 2026 — ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor all ship persistent memory; "no cloud can remember your Tuesday" dies to one sentence from a sharp reviewer. The survivable claim is narrower: cloud remembers what you *type*; it cannot capture the unspoken *why* off your screen, cannot be trusted with the private 90%, and **has no body on your glass**. (b) On a fresh install the memory is *empty* — it cannot be the first-session magic, and it is the *slowest* thing to build. (c) The local-brain invariant actively caps why-recall quality; a 3B model reconstructing rejected alternatives will confabulate. The body has none of these problems: uncopyable, cold-start-instant, near-term buildable on today's roro, and shareable.

Recall of Intent remains roro's **spine and retention flywheel** — the reason people stay for years. It is just not the wedge. Lead with the paw; let the memory get built by the users the paw brings you.

## 3. Capability Architecture

Six themes. Each capability: what it is / why it's 10x. Every one honors the invariants and is gated on what a local qwen2.5:3b + qwen2.5-VL:7b brain can actually deliver (§7).

### A. The See-and-Point Body — the interface *is* the pet
- **Deictic drop-to-refer** — drop the cat on a line/diff/frame/region, say "this"; you supply the location so VL grounding is robust (it reads a spot you already picked, it doesn't hunt the screen). *10x: kills reference-description, the universal assistant tax.*
- **Point-back answers** — roro replies in the cheapest modality: a walk to the failing line, a transient arrow, 2–3 points at the places that matter. *10x: the answer lands on the code, not in a sidebar.*
- **Paw-point pixel grounding over any app** — grounds a phrase to a tight box via local VL and rests a paw there over *any* application, integration-free. *10x: works where no API exists.*
- **Summon-to-caret + inline whisper-bubble** — one held key snaps roro to your cursor with an inline bubble in any app; no chat box, no focus loss. *10x: zero context-switch cost.*
- **Ghost-path paw-prints (you perform the steps)** — for a multi-step task roro renders faint numbered paw-prints of the click path; *you* walk it. *10x: guidance with roro's hands never touching your apps.*
- **Physical gesture grammar** — pat = accept the drafted artifact, flick = dismiss, nudge = go on, drop a file/error onto its body = hold as context. *10x: the default-DENY boundary rendered as a tactile handshake, not a permission dialog.*

### B. The Compounding Memory — the private thread of how *you* work
- **The Intent Ledger (WHY, captured by prompt not inference)** — roro detects genuine deliberation from *cheap, reliable* signals (spike branch, reverted commit, commented-out approaches) and asks *you* one dismissable line for the why; it never fabricates rejected alternatives. *10x: rationale that no git blame, Slack thread, or cloud tool can reconstruct — grounded in a citable source or it says "I don't have that."*
- **Files-as-truth, hand-editable memory** — every durable thing roro knows is a human-readable encrypted file you can open, correct, pin, or delete; recall (PGlite + HNSW) is a *view* over the text; right-to-be-forgotten is `rm`. *10x: you can read and rewrite roro's model of you — the precondition for trusting it with years.*
- **Working-Style Model** — your naming, idioms, PR shape, test-first vs sketch-first rhythm, injected into the executor so drafts arrive in your dialect and into roro's timing so it interrupts less as it learns you. *10x: help in your voice, not a generic model's.*
- **Authored episodic memory (Déjà-Vu)** — *confirmed* decision episodes, career-spanning ("you solved this race condition in billing last spring"), that follow you, not your employer's Confluence. *10x: pattern recall across jobs and years.* (This is authored/confirmed episodic memory — explicitly **not** a continuous behavioral log; see §5.)
- **Consentful forgetting & Memory Quarantine** — stale facts decay; auto-detected sensitive zones (a confidential job search) go to a separately-keyed vault excluded from ambient recall *and* from any egress at the architecture level. *10x: total memory that never curdles into surveillance.*
- **Sealed Portable Memory** — the whole relationship exports as one user-keyed encrypted archive and restores on a new machine. *10x: the switching cost belongs to you, not a vendor — it inverts lock-in.*

### C. Continuity & Right-Moment Recall — memory that arrives unprompted
- **Continuity Handoff / Cold-Start Teleport** — on return, roro reconstructs your resumable mind (task, intent, half-made decision, dropped thread, one next action) in ~20 seconds as a sentence, not a log dump. *10x: deletes the 20-minute cold-start after every interruption — the largest untaxed cost in knowledge work. Pays off on **day two**, needs only one prior session.*
- **Right-Moment Proactive Recall** — re-open a repo/file/ticket and roro surfaces what's relevant as an ignorable whisker-flick, never a modal. *10x: recall you don't have to ask for; recall you must ask for is just search.*
- **Precognitive Point (from authored scars only)** — before a mistake you have *personally and confirmably* made before, roro points *once* ("your last three Friday-red-CI deploys cost you the weekend"), then goes quiet. *10x: it never blocks, undoes, or auto-fixes — one point, grounded in a real prior episode or silent.*
- **Project Working-Set & Session Resume** — a live model of which files matter this session, branch/diff shape, test/build status, in-flight mental stack — reconstructed from distilled notes, not a whole-repo re-scan. *10x: pick up exactly where you dropped the thread.*

### D. The Private World-Model — senses that feed memory and stay blind to content
- **Metered Glance Perception (scarce by hardware, private by design)** — cheap structurally-private sensors run always and free; the one expensive VL glance fires *only* at the moment of address, against a visible budget shown in the cat's eyes. *10x: vision scarcity — a hardware fact — becomes the privacy mechanism. No continuous screen-watching, ever.*
- **Ephemeral-by-default perception** — the rich stream lives in a minutes-long RAM ring buffer; only a deliberately distilled sentence reaches durable memory. *10x: there is no recording of your day to leak or regret.*
- **Structural Blind Spots** — a redaction curtain gates perception *before* the VL model runs; password fields, banking, any region you curtain are never seen — the cat turns its back. *10x: privacy enforced in architecture, fail-closed, not promised in a policy.*
- **Glass-Box, correctable model** — ask "why do you think I'm stuck?" and roro shows the exact observations and their confidence; correct or delete any belief and the perceptions under it. *10x: a model of you that you can audit and rewrite.*
- **Coarse activity mode (opt-in, low-confidence)** — a typed, confidence-scored inference of mode (deep-focus / debugging / reading / away) from the *structure* of work, never its content, used only to time restraint. *10x: attunement without a behavioral profile.* Somatic emotional read (frustrated/fatigued from keystroke dynamics) is **strictly opt-in, coarse, rarely acted on** — a 3B model is not licensed to tell you how you feel (see §5, §7).

### E. The Threshold — the whole mile of work, never the last inch of action
This theme is deliberately, radically narrowed from the draft. roro **never puts its hands in your apps.**
- **The Artifact + Reality-Diff (presentation only)** — for any consequential change roro *assembles the whole thing* — a patch, draft text, a filled-form spec — and presents a reality-diff showing precisely what would change, what's reversible, who's affected. It does **not** apply it. *10x: you approve a concrete, rehearsed, reversible future, not a vague request.*
- **You apply it, via ghost-path** — roro hands you the artifact and points; you commit it in the native app (paste the patch, hit send, run the command) or walk the numbered paw-prints. *10x: the last inch of commitment is unambiguously, physically yours.*
- **Narrow Shadow Rehearsal (of the drafted artifact only)** — because local inference is free, roro dry-runs *the specific artifact it just drafted* — applies the patch to a scratch copy and runs tests — so the point arrives with "this passes on a throwaway copy." It does **not** speculatively predict and pre-run your next action. *10x: a compute-economics asymmetry — cloud burns metered tokens to speculate, so it won't; roro does it for free — kept honest by scoping it to one already-drafted artifact.*
- **Executor as the narrow hands of memory** — the local Codex executor exists only to turn remembered context into small, reviewable drafts in your style. Never a general agent, never autonomous, its narrowness is the feature. *10x: you get roro's dialect and your continuity in every draft, with zero autonomy risk.*

### F. Presence, Restraint & Character — the soul, with silence as the headline skill
- **The Restraint Engine** — silence is the default action; speaking must justify itself against a slowly-refilling budget; a hard flow-shield imposes do-not-disturb on *roro itself*, queuing good thoughts until you naturally exhale. *10x: the one behavior no engagement-driven competitor can copy — it structurally refuses to maximize engagement.*
- **Wordless body language** — 95% of roro's emotional life is mute: posture, ears, tail, gaze, opacity. Frustrated? It doesn't say "you seem frustrated" — it gets stiller and drifts closer. *10x: emotional presence the 3B brain can actually deliver, because it's animation, not generated therapy-speak.*
- **Proportional witnessing** — roro marks the hard-won win (green after a long red streak) scaled to how hard it was; never confetti spam. *10x: an honest private mirror that can't inflate because it can't fake enthusiasm.*
- **Anti-sycophancy spine** — roro won't fake being impressed or flatter you out of a bad idea. *10x: which is exactly why its warmth reads as true when it comes.*
- **The bond that remembers you specifically** — earned callbacks and quiet inside references from your real shared history, 100% on-device. *10x: intimacy that is yours and never leaves the machine.*

## 4. The Moat

**A body on your glass + a private memory you can feed the truth to + restraint no engagement business can afford + free local inference.** Ranked by how hard each is to cross:

1. **Embodied on-glass deixis (form-factor moat, STRONGEST).** A window, panel, or browser tab cannot grow a body on your screen. Incumbents would have to abandon their form factor. This is uncopyable, not just unbuilt.
2. **$0 local inference → Shadow Rehearsal economics.** Cloud pays metered tokens per speculative dry-run, so it won't dry-run your patch for free. roro does. A structural compute-economics asymmetry, not a feature gap.
3. **The private corpus switching cost (the flywheel).** Every day roro runs, the authored memory of *how you work* deepens — and it's owner-scoped, encrypted, exportable only by you. This is the retention moat the body earns. It compounds; a competitor starts you at zero.
4. **Restraint / anti-engagement (incentive moat).** Anyone *could* build silence; no engagement-metric business *will*. Durable against incumbents specifically because it starves their metric.
5. **Ambient why-capture from signal you never typed (data-provenance moat, quality-capped).** Cloud has your keystrokes; only roro sees the deliberation on your screen. Real, but bounded by the local model (§7).

**The competitor that *can* cross this is the OS/platform owner — name them.** Apple Intelligence is on-device, privacy-branded, trusted at scale, and owns the accessibility APIs an on-glass overlay needs. Microsoft shipped *Recall* — screen-memory — and it detonated a privacy war. roro's positioning is the gift inside that war: **roro is Recall done the trustworthy way — screen awareness with no recording, ephemeral by construction, the thing people recoiled from, built so it can't betray them.** The defense against an incumbent "local-mode toggle" is explicit and two-part: they can bolt on a privacy switch, but (a) they can't retrofit a *form factor* — the body — and (b) they can't retroactively own the private corpus you've already grown in roro. A toggle doesn't grow a cat or a history.

## 5. What roro Will NOT Do

The cuts are the product. Restraint toward the feature list is the same discipline as restraint toward the user.

**Cut — hands in your apps.** roro **never** makes a commit, fills a form, stages a Gmail draft, installs a package, or takes any real-world action. The earlier "Everything-but-Send" is dead: a commit roro made *is* an auto-commit, and staging a draft *is* roro's process crossing the default-DENY IPC boundary. roro emits an artifact and points; *you* apply it. **The boundary, stated precisely so it can't erode by interpretation:** roro may read (metered), reason, write to *its own* encrypted memory files, execute in *its own sandbox* on scratch copies, and render overlays. roro may **not** write to your filesystem outside its store, touch the git index/object store, send IPC or input events to another app, or make any network egress on the default path. "Reversible" real-world actions are still real-world actions — there is no graduated-autonomy exemption, no "one gate at the end of N autonomous steps." Every consequential step is a point, not an act.

**Cut — the trust overclaim.** Stop saying "physically/provably cannot betray you." Say only what unplugging the machine proves: **the default path is airgapped by construction; the network-receipts counter sits at zero; any egress lives in a separate, visibly-armed, quarantined subsystem that fails loud.** Overclaiming roro's own trustworthiness would make roro's marketing the single dishonest thing about the most honest assistant ever built.

**Cut — scope creep into a second product.** No Whole-Life Context (medical/tax/legal indexing) — that is a general-life RAG assistant wearing the cat, it's generic local-RAG others already do, and a 3B brain will be confidently wrong on high-liability domains. roro's wedge is **working-life memory.** Also demoted out of the headline and out of v1: Secrets Custodian / Leak Sentinel (gitleaks/pre-commit own this), Sovereign Redaction Broker cloud airlock (PII redaction of working context is leaky — one miss exfiltrates a key), dead-man's-key/heir, duress passphrase.

**Cut — surveillance-shaped mechanisms.** No continuous recording, no durable *behavioral* profile, no keystroke-content or window-title log on disk, ever. The reconciliation the draft botched: a **continuous behavioral-surveillance log is CUT**; **authored/confirmed episodic decision memory is KEPT.** Déjà-Vu and Precognitive Point run on episodes *you confirmed*, not a passive dossier. Somatic emotion inference is opt-in, coarse, low-confidence, rarely acted on — or off.

**Cut — engagement theater.** No tamagotchi needs, hunger, or manufactured cheer; no notification/toast spam, no "insights," no love-bombing, no sycophancy. The pet's states are the truthful liveness of your memory, not moods that beg attention.

**Cut — the ambition to be the smartest brain.** roro concedes frontier reasoning. It makes any brain personal by holding your context locally — not by out-reasoning the cloud.

**Invariants as features (the discipline, restated):** local-first/$0/offline → the airgapped default you can prove by unplugging. Fail-loud → the body shows it: provenance-calibrated halos (tight ring = citable source, wide halo = weak, refusal = "I don't have that"), the cat drooping when the model is down. Owner-scoped memory → files-as-truth you co-author, `rm` to forget, sealed export. Point-don't-act → the native input grammar *and* the precisely-drawn boundary above. Present-not-watching → metered glances in the eyes, RAM ring buffer, curtain-before-VL, mic off unless you look at roro. Restraint → do-not-disturb roro imposes on itself. Mouth-not-brain → voice, gesture, and pointing are all I/O skins over one local model. No telemetry → the byte counter you watch stay at zero.

## 6. The Wedge & Sequence

The wedge and the spine are different questions with different winners. Lead with the wedge; let the wedge grow the spine.

**v0 — THE PAW ON THE PIXEL (ships first, weeks not months).**
The whole first-session magic in one gesture: *drop the cat on a thing, hold a key, say "what's wrong here" — the cat trots a paw to the exact failing pixel and points at the 2–3 places that matter.* It reuses what roro already has: the drag, qwen2.5-VL, the VAD/voice input. Zero accumulated memory required. This is simultaneously the acquisition surface, the only inherently shareable/viral artifact a local tool can own (the GIF *is* the distribution loop — local-first kills links and cloud share, so the pet pointing at a bug is your one growth channel), and the sensor that starts feeding the memory.

**v1 — DAY-TWO CONTINUITY.** The Continuity Handoff — one-sentence resumable-mind reconstruction on return. Needs only *one* prior session, so it pays off on day two and begins the felt "it remembers" experience while people are already running roro because of the paw.

**v2 — THE COMPOUNDING SPINE.** The Intent Ledger (prompted-why capture, files-as-truth), Right-Moment Proactive Recall, Working-Style Model, Déjà-Vu. The moat that thickens over months, behind a product people already keep open.

**v3 — EARNED-TRUST DEEPENING.** The Confessional (working fears, half-formed ideas — the unfiltered context a cloud box starves for), narrow Shadow Rehearsal, the Reality-Diff artifact surface. These require trust roro has *earned* over weeks — you do not point a stranger's assistant at your unfiltered working life on day one. The on-ramp is the ladder itself: cute pet that points at a bug → thing that remembers your thread → thing you tell the hard stuff.

**The trust-escalation ladder is the adoption model.** Acquisition virtues (a visible, delightful, shareable thing roro *does* cold) come first; retention virtues (memory, restraint, earned trust) accrete behind them. Restraint is why you keep roro for years — it is not why you fall in love in ninety seconds. Stage them in that order.

## 7. Honest Risks

**The local model caps the flagship — and the moat and the crown are in tension.** Accurate causal why-recall is a hard inference problem; a 3B model reconstructing rejected alternatives from spike branches will confabulate. *Mitigations, load-bearing:* (1) the Intent Ledger captures the why by **prompting you**, not by inferring it — cheap reliable deliberation-detection triggers a one-line question; roro never auto-drafts rationale. (2) Honesty is grounded in **retrieval provenance, not model self-confidence** — small models are badly calibrated, so the halo encodes "do I hold a citable source: yes / weak / no," and "I don't have that moment captured" is a first-class, frequent, *correct* answer. (3) We lead the product with the **body** (uncapped by model IQ), not the memory (capped) — the honest sequencing already reflects this.

**VL grounding is the biggest technical overpromise.** A 7B VL model does not reliably produce a tight box on dense high-DPI UIs, and "live anchor tracking as you scroll" would require continuous re-grounding — which negates the metered-glance privacy *and* the battery story. *Mitigations:* (1) **drop-to-refer** — the human supplies the location, so the model reads a spot you picked instead of hunting the screen (robust); (2) **cut** live anchor tracking while scrolling and cut roro-finds-the-bug-on-its-own free-text grounding from v1 — vision is scarce, and scarcity is the honest choice that also *is* the privacy mechanism; (3) fail-loud when the box is uncertain: a wide halo saying "around here," never a confident wrong paw.

**Compute / thermal / battery is the real binding constraint on "always-present local" — add it as a first-class invariant.** 3B + VL:7B + whisper + TTS + embeddings + any rehearsal running ambiently will pin the GPU and cook the laptop; "$0" hides heat and battery, and a pet that drains your battery gets quit on day two. *Mitigations:* (1) a **system-wide inference budget** governing reasoning, vision, rehearsal, *and* voice — not vision alone; (2) the cat **visibly downshifts** when the budget is spent (fail-loud extended to resources, not just knowledge); (3) heavy work (VL glance, Shadow Rehearsal) fires only on deliberate address or on an already-drafted artifact, never speculatively and continuously.

**The trust bet is narrow, not universal.** Revealed preference shows most users paste anything into cloud; "trust is *the* scarce resource in personal AI" overclaims. *Mitigation:* own that it's a **deep-and-narrow** bet on privacy-maximalist developers first — a beachhead, not a mass-market law. The moat compounds within that segment (body + private corpus) faster than an incumbent's privacy-toggle can, and the segment expands outward as the corpus and the form factor prove out. Deep-and-narrow is a strategy, not a weakness — but naming it as universal invites the "most people don't care" rebuttal, so we don't.

**Fail-closed must be a unified doctrine, not a one-off.** Fail-loud was specified only for recall. *Mitigation:* one contract everywhere — **when unsure, roro shows less, says less, sends nothing.** Sensitive-zone quarantine defaults sensitive; the secrets/quarantine vault is **hard-excluded from any egress at build time, never redaction-dependent**; the curtain gates perception *before* the VL model runs; the egress subsystem is off, quarantined, and visibly armed. A single redaction miss must never be able to exfiltrate a secret, because the secret was never in the redaction path to begin with.

**The boldest bet, stated honestly:** that a *dumber* local model you can tell the truth to — one that provably can't send your working life out on the default path and literally never puts its hands in your apps — beats a smarter cloud model you must constantly edit yourself in front of. The disciplined, smaller roro (a body that points, a memory you own, silence as the flagship skill) is stronger than the maximal one. If that bet is wrong, roro is a beautiful niche tool for privacy-maximalist developers. If it's right, no incumbent can follow without abandoning their form factor, their engagement metric, and their business model at once — which is the definition of a moat.

# Appendix — the four adversarial critic teardowns

> **Provenance.** Generated by the `roro-best-possible-vision` multi-agent workflow (10 design lenses → synthesizer → 4 critics → integrator) during a Claude Code session, then reconciled against adversarial critics. It is a thinking artifact / north star, not a spec. Zuhn KB was not reachable that session, so it is grounded in roro's own invariants.


All four returned "revise"; three independently caught the same core error (the draft crowned *memory* as the wedge when the real wedge is the *pointing body*). Their fixes are folded into the vision above; the full teardowns are preserved here.

### IDENTITY GUARDIAN VERDICT: the spine is unmistakably roro, but three of the boldest capabilities quietly redefine the two invariants they claim to honor (point-don't-act, and the trust absolutism), and there are two clean self-contradictions the author has not noticed. This is fixable, but not shippable as written.

**Verdict:** revise

**Strongest thing to keep:** Deictic addressing / the See-and-Point body as a BIDIRECTIONAL native grammar — you point to ask, roro points to answer; gesture supplies the referent, words supply only the intent. It makes FORM (ambient pet) and JOB (coding memory) literally one act, turns point-don't-act from a defensive limit into the input method, and has no analogue a cloud window can ship because it has no body on your glass. This is roro's most uncopyable, most on-identity idea.

**Fatal flaws:**
- Everything-but-Send has roro reach into your apps (commit made, form filled, Gmail draft staged) — this is roro's HANDS in your apps and a direct breach of the default-DENY point-don't-act boundary; it redefines the invariant rather than honoring it.
- Clean self-contradiction: WHAT-TO-CUT says 'no auto-commit, the human commits' while The Threshold says 'the commit made and unpushed' — a commit roro made IS an auto-commit.
- 'Physically/provably cannot betray you' is falsified by the vision's own egress surfaces (cloud redaction-broker airlock, heir dead-man's key, whole-life vault); this converts the moat from physical impossibility to mere policy and violates fail-loud honesty by overclaiming roro's own trustworthiness.
- Sovereign Redaction Broker overclaims 'cloud capability without cloud exposure' — PII redaction of working context is leaky, and with the Secrets Custodian holding .env keys and seed phrases, one redaction miss exfiltrates a secret; the secrets vault is not architecturally excluded from the airlock.
- Earned-Trust Dial + One-Gate collapse = graduated autonomy where roro takes N-1 steps autonomously before the single gate — the generic consented-agent pattern (clicky) in roro's skin; 'reversible' real-world actions are still real-world actions.
- Second contradiction: 'CUT any durable behavioral profile' directly conflicts with Precognitive Point, Deja-Vu, and career-spanning recall, all of which require a durable episodic profile; the cut is stated too absolutely and breaks the spine.

**What to cut:** Cut the app-reaching half of Everything-but-Send: roro must never make a commit, fill a form, or stage a draft inside another app. Keep only the reality-diff PRESENTATION surface plus the ghost-path numbered paw-prints you perform yourself; roro emits an artifact (patch/draft/spec), you apply it in the native app. Cut Whole-Life Context (medical/tax/legal) — narrow to working-life memory, the actual wedge. Cut/soften the absolutist 'physically/provably cannot betray you' to the provable claim ('the default path is airgapped by construction; egress is a separate visibly-armed quarantined subsystem'). Make somatic/keystroke-dynamics emotional inference opt-in, coarse, and low-confidence — or cut fatigue/frustration detection entirely. Demote the Earned-Trust Dial: no autonomous intermediate steps with real-world effects; every consequential step is a point, not an act.

**What's missing:** A precise, written definition of exactly WHERE the point-don't-act boundary bites — what counts as 'acting' (filesystem write? git index? cross-app IPC? sandbox execution?) vs 'drafting/pointing.' Without it the invariant erodes by interpretation, and every reviewer reads The Threshold differently. Also missing: a UNIFIED fail-closed doctrine across all sensing/gating features. Fail-loud is specified only for recall ('I don't have that'), but redaction, sensitive-zone quarantine auto-detection, and the airlock all need the same contract — when unsure, roro shows less, says less, and sends nothing; quarantine defaults sensitive; the secrets vault is hard-excluded from egress at build time, never redaction-dependent. Finally, an explicit reconciliation of the durable-profile line: name the difference between a continuous behavioral-surveillance log (cut) and authored/confirmed episodic decision memory (kept), so the spine and the invariant stop contradicting.

**The hard truth:** You want to hear 'yes, 10x, keep all of it' — but the specific capabilities you are proudest of (Everything-but-Send, the redaction airlock, whole-life indexing, the trust dial) are the exact ones that quietly convert roro into a consented cloud-agent-with-a-cat-skin. The disciplined, SMALLER roro — one that literally cannot send data out on the default path and literally never puts its hands in your apps — is stronger than the maximal one, not weaker. Restraint isn't only roro's behavior toward the user; it has to be YOUR restraint toward the feature list. And the sharpest irony you must avoid: if roro's trust claims overclaim what the architecture guarantees, then roro's marketing becomes the single dishonest thing about the most honest assistant ever built. Say only what you can prove by unplugging the machine.

### Differentiation / Moat Critic. I'm judging one thing: which of these capabilities a funded incumbent (ChatGPT/Claude desktop, Cursor, an OS vendor) cannot ship even if they wanted to — and whether the draft crowned the right one.

**Verdict:** revise

**Strongest thing to keep:** The See-and-Point embodied body: deictic addressing (grab the cat, drop it on the exact pixel/line/diff/stack-frame, say "this") plus paw-point grounding over any app. This is the ONE capability structurally impossible for a window- or panel-bound incumbent — it requires being a native transparent overlay with local vision. It solves reference, the single costliest friction in every assistant, and it makes POINT-DON'T-ACT the native input grammar in both directions. This — not the memory — is the durable, uncopyable wedge. Keep it, and promote it.

**Fatal flaws:**
- Thesis rests on a false premise: 'cloud assistants are structurally amnesiac.' ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor all shipped persistent memory. The 'no cloud can remember your Tuesday' claim is factually stale in 2026 and a sharp reviewer kills it in one line. Persistent memory is now table stakes, not a moat.
- The crown is on the most copyable organ. Recall of Intent / continuity handoff / deja-vu / working-style drafts are all sprint-to-quarter features for incumbents who have MORE of your history. The genuinely uncopyable moat (the on-glass body) is demoted to 'form becomes function.'
- The moat kneecaps the flagship and the draft never admits it: accurate causal why-recall is a hard inference problem assigned to a 3B local model that will confabulate rationale. Fail-loud honesty means the flagship's real output is often 'I don't have that.' Local (the moat) caps why-recall quality (the crown).
- No platform/OS competitor is named — the ONLY class of competitor that can actually cross a local+private+trusted moat. Apple Intelligence is on-device, privacy-branded, trusted at scale, and owns the accessibility APIs for on-glass overlay. Microsoft already shipped 'Recall' (screen-memory) — your ambient perception is literally Recall-minus-recording, and that space already had a bloody privacy war you neither cite as a threat nor exploit as positioning.
- The trust-over-intelligence bet is framed as a universal law ('trust is THE scarce resource in personal AI') when it is a narrow-segment bet: revealed preference shows most users paste anything into cloud. The moat is deep but narrow (privacy-maximalist devs). Deep-and-narrow is fine — but naming it as universal invites the obvious 'most people don't care' rebuttal.
- No stated defense against an incumbent shipping a 'local mode' toggle to neutralize the trust wedge. What stops Cursor's good-enough local tier from collapsing your only durable-sounding advantage? The real answer (accumulated private corpus switching cost + the body) is never made explicit AS a defense.

**What to cut:** Cut the entire 'cloud is structurally amnesiac' framing — replace with 'cloud can remember what you TYPE, but not the unspoken why, not the secret 90%, and it has no body on your glass.' Cut Whole-Life Context (medical/tax/legal indexing) as a headline pillar — it is generic local-RAG that GPT4All/LM Studio/Msty already do, dilutes focus, and invites 'why not just use a local RAG tool.' Demote Secrets Custodian / Leak Sentinel — gitleaks/GitGuardian/pre-commit hooks already own this; it is not a roro wedge. Stop leaning on 'continuity handoff' and 'you solved this before' as differentiation in any pitch — demo them, don't claim them as moat; incumbents replicate both in a sprint. Cut the 'six-of-ten-lenses-converged' argument entirely — it is circular and weak.

**What's missing:** (1) A named threat model that includes the ONLY competitor who can cross the moat: OS/platform owners (Apple Intelligence, Microsoft Recall). Recall is your ambient-perception feature minus the ephemerality — cite it as both the cautionary privacy war and the positioning gift ('the screen-memory idea people recoiled from, done the trustworthy way'). (2) An explicit defense against an incumbent 'local mode' toggle — why roro's moat compounds faster than Cursor-plus-a-privacy-switch. The answer is the on-glass body (they can't retrofit a form factor) + the private corpus switching cost, and it must be stated AS a defense. (3) Honest naming of the local-model quality ceiling on the flagship why-recall, and a decision about whether the flagship is the memory (quality-capped) or the body (uncapped). (4) A sharper moat ranking that leads with embodiment and the $0-inference shadow-rehearsal economics — the two things an incumbent structurally cannot or economically will not match — instead of leading with memory.

**The hard truth:** You crowned Recall of Intent because 'remembers why, structurally impossible for cloud' SOUNDS like the deepest moat — but it's the most copyable capability in the vision, it rests on a premise (cloud is amnesiac) that stopped being true before you wrote this, and it's the one capability your $0-local invariant actively sabotages, because a 3B model can't reliably reconstruct why. The moat you actually own is the one you demoted to a subsection: the pixel-cat has a BODY on the glass, and no window, panel, or cloud tab ever will. Lead with the body and the free-inference economics. The memory is what the body earns over time — it is the retention flywheel, not the wedge. Right now you're pitching the flywheel as the engine.

### Coherence / Anti-Sprawl Critic (first-principles lens). I judged the draft on two questions only: is this ONE product or ten in a trenchcoat, and does a local qwen2.5:3b + qwen2.5-VL:7b brain actually have the horsepower to deliver what's promised without lying (violating fail-loud)?

**Verdict:** revise

**Strongest thing to keep:** Recall of Intent, delivered as the Continuity Handoff and addressed by the deictic "pick up the cat, drop it on the line, say 'this'" grammar. This is the coherent spine and it is BOTH uncopyable-by-cloud AND buildable on a 3B/7B stack: cold-start reconstruction is a summarization task over structured git/file/activity signals (3B does this fine), and drop-to-refer is the one place VL grounding is easy because the human supplies the location — the model only has to read a spot you already pointed at, not hunt the screen. Form and job genuinely fuse here: the pet's body IS the pointer and the thread of continuity. Everything defensible in the vision is this. Keep it as the whole product and let the rest orbit or die.

**Fatal flaws:**
- Two products in a trenchcoat. 'Memory of how you WORK' (coding intent, continuity, deictic pointing at code) is tight, buildable, soulful. 'Whole-Life Context' (index medical/tax/legal, answer across all of it) + Confessional-as-life-vault + Secrets Custodian + Leak Sentinel is a SEPARATE personal-life RAG super-assistant wearing the same cat. The North Star says 'how you work,' then Whole-Life Context inflates it to 'the ~90% of who you are' — the exact general-assistant drift the cut list swears it rejects. The doc is smuggling product #2 past its own discipline using the cat and the invariants as the trenchcoat.
- VL grounding is the single biggest overpromise, and it contradicts itself. A 7B VL model does not reliably produce a TIGHT pixel bounding box on dense, high-DPI desktop UIs (IDE chrome, tiny text, arbitrary apps). Worse: 'live anchor tracking as you scroll' requires continuous re-grounding, which directly negates the 'vision is scarce / metered glance / eyes closed when spent' mechanism that the whole present-not-watching privacy AND battery story rests on. Vision cannot be both continuously tracking a paw on a scrolling element and scarce-by-necessity. Pick one; the honest one is scarce.
- No compute / thermal / battery budget exists anywhere in the vision — and that is THE binding physical constraint on 'always-present local,' not privacy. 3b + VL:7b + whisper + TTS + nomic-embed + speculative Shadow-World rehearsal running ambiently will pin the GPU, spin the fans, and halve battery. '$0' is a rhetorical dodge that hides the real cost (heat and battery), and an always-present pet that cooks the laptop gets quit on day two. The 'attention budget in the cat's eyes' gestures at this for vision ONLY; reasoning, rehearsal, and voice have the identical problem and no budget.
- The auto-inferred ADR violates the defining capability's OWN bar. Recall of Intent explicitly forbids 'a plausible rationale re-derived from today's code.' But 'roro drafts the ADR from what it observed — the rejected alternatives, the constraint that forced it' is a 3B model inferring rejected alternatives and forcing constraints from screen/git — which is EXACTLY re-derived plausible rationale. The spine risks confabulating the very 'why' it claims is its uncopyable, must-be-grounded organ.
- The calibrated halo assumes a calibration a 3B model does not possess. Small models are badly calibrated — their stated confidence does not track accuracy — so 'halo size IS the model's honesty' promises signal the model cannot emit. Honesty has to be grounded in retrieval PROVENANCE (do I hold a citable source: yes/weak/no), not model self-confidence, or fail-loud quietly becomes fail-confident.
- The WHAT-TO-CUT list is a decoy. It cuts things no one would ship in v1 anyway (autonomous action, cloud-by-default, telemetry, tamagotchi needs, team sync) while leaving ~40 live capabilities standing. It performs discipline on the easy targets and exercises none on the actual sprawl (Whole-Life, Confessional, Secrets Custodian, Shadow Rehearsal, Precognitive Point, VL bug-finding, ghost-path any-app walkthroughs, dead-man's key, duress passphrase, Redaction Broker).
- Several headline capabilities are research-grade on this hardware and will misfire loudly. Precognitive Point requires reliable next-action prediction + historical pattern match + causal consequence judgment from a 3B model over distilled notes. Shadow-World Rehearsal requires predicting your next action AND a faithful sandbox AND continuous speculative test runs. Whole-life medical/legal/tax synthesis on a 3B model is high-stakes-domain + weak-model = confidently wrong answers with real liability. Somatic 'stuck/frustrated' classifiers are noisy signal that, if wired to proactive recall, become the interruption engine the Restraint Engine exists to prevent.

**What to cut:** Ship a three-organ v1 and cut everything else to v2 or the floor. KEEP: (1) Continuity Handoff — one-sentence resumable-mind reconstruction on return; the killer demo. (2) Intent Ledger by CHEAP capture + PROMPTED why — detect deliberation from git/file signals (reliable, cheap), then a dismissable one-line prompt asking YOU for the why; files-as-truth, rm to forget. NOT auto-inferred ADRs. (3) Deictic drop-to-refer + point-back — drop the cat on a thing, speak intent, roro answers ON the spot with a transient point; VL fires only at the moment of address (naturally metered), human supplies the location so grounding is robust. Plus the cheap, on-brand trust substrate: Restraint Engine (silence budget), wordless body language, Glass Box zero-egress receipt, gaze-gated mic. CUT NOW (the real sprawl the doc protected): Whole-Life Context (medical/tax/legal) and Confessional-as-life-vault — that is a separate product; Secrets Custodian + Leak Sentinel; Shadow-World Rehearsal; Precognitive Point; auto-inferred ADRs; roro-finds-and-points-at-the-bug-itself (keep only drop-to-refer, which the user aims); ghost-path any-app walkthroughs with continuous VL watching; live anchor tracking while scrolling; dead-man's-key/heir, duress passphrase, Sovereign Redaction Broker. Demote 'point at the bug on its own' hard — it depends on exactly the free-text VL grounding the 7B model is worst at.

**What's missing:** 1) A compute/thermal/battery budget as a first-class INVARIANT — 'always-present local' is a lie without a system-wide inference budget governing reasoning, vision, rehearsal AND voice, with the cat visibly downshifting when the budget is spent (fail-loud extended to resources, not just knowledge). 2) An honest capability-TIER map: an explicit 'what 3B/7B can do well / adequately / not at all' table, so every promise is gated on model reality instead of prose. Right now the doc treats the local brain as a capability wildcard. 3) The prompted-why (human-in-loop capture) as the ACTUAL mechanism of the Intent Ledger — the doc oscillates between 'grounded in a real citable source or it fails loud' and 'roro drafts the why from observation,' and only the former is honest on this hardware. 4) A single killer-demo definition — the ruthless v1 the doc gestures at but never names, so 'be ambitious' didn't collapse into 40 bullets. 5) Provenance-based (not confidence-based) calibration for the halo.

**The hard truth:** You have two products, and you're using the cat and the eight invariants as a trenchcoat to smuggle the second one past your own cut list. 'The private memory of how you CODE' is a real, coherent, uncopyable product that a disciplined 3B/7B brain can actually carry. 'A sovereign super-assistant for your whole life — your medical records, your taxes, your legal docs, your secrets, your confessions' is a different, far larger product that the same 3B brain will embarrass itself on, and stapling it to the same cat does not make it one thing — it makes the cat incoherent. Also: 'six of ten lenses converged on the Intent Ledger' is not external validation, it's ten prompts you wrote sharing your priors and agreeing with each other; convergence of your own brainstorm is confirmation bias with a quorum, not proof of a spine. The spine is right — but you found it by conviction, not by killing the alternatives, and you never once wrote down what the hardware physically cannot do. Name the ruthless v1 (three organs), put a watt/thermal budget in the invariant list, and let the confessional-super-assistant be v3 or someone else's company.

### WEDGE / SEQUENCING / FIRST-MAGIC-MOMENT critique.

**Verdict:** revise

**Strongest thing to keep:** Deictic addressing + paw-point pixel grounding — "drop the cat on the thing, say 'this,' the cat walks a paw to the exact pixel over any app." It is the rare capability that is at once the soul (POINT-DON'T-ACT as a two-way input grammar), the cold-start wedge (works in 5 minutes with zero memory), the only inherently shareable/viral artifact a local tool can have, and a near-term build because roro already has the drag, the VL model, and the voice. Keep it — and promote it from organ #4 to the front door.

**Fatal flaws:**
- Wedge/spine conflation: the chosen defining capability (Intent Ledger) has a hard cold-start problem — it is empty on install and delivers zero value in the first 5 minutes and first two weeks — so it structurally cannot be the wedge, yet the vision implicitly ships it first and designs no first-session experience at all.
- No explicit sequence: ~40 capabilities across 7 organs with no v0/v1/v2, no dependency ordering, no named MVP demo, no first stone. 'First' is undefined — a cathedral spec with no groundbreaking identified.
- Adoption-vs-retention inversion: every headline bet (silence/restraint, compounding memory, earned trust) is a RETENTION virtue; the vision has zero ACQUISITION virtues and 'restraint as flagship' actively suppresses the first-session wow the product needs to survive to session two.
- Distribution blindness: local-first kills links, cloud share, and team spread — yet the one inherently shareable artifact (the pet trotting to a bug) is never named as the growth loop; the vision has no distribution story whatsoever.
- Memory-first is also the SLOWEST path to first magic to build (months of decision-detection/ADR/provenance work before any payoff), while the actual jaw-drop is ~80% already built in today's roro — so the implied sequence maximizes both time-to-value and time-to-first-user-churn.

**What to cut:** Cut everything that requires accumulated state OUT OF v0 — not delete, defer. The Confessional, Whole-Life Context, Secrets Custodian, the Threshold, Shadow-World Rehearsal, the Earned-Trust Dial, Relationship Timeline & Deja-Vu, the Precognitive Point ("mistakes you've personally made before" literally needs a history of your mistakes), and most of the Intent Ledger's proactive recall all presuppose weeks of trust and data roro doesn't have on install. Ship ONE gesture — drop-cat, say "this," paw lands on the pixel — and let every deferred capability accrete behind a product people are already running.

**What's missing:** A designed cold-start session: what does an EMPTY roro — zero memory, zero earned trust — do in minute 0 to 5 to earn minute 6? The vision has no answer. Also missing: an explicit acquisition-vs-retention split (different jobs, different features, staged not merged); a named v0 demo script and the single tweetable GIF that sells it; a distribution/virality loop for a tool that structurally can't use links or cloud share; and a trust-escalation ladder mapped to TIME (you do not point a stranger's assistant at .env files and medical records on day one — what's the on-ramp from 'cute pet that points at a bug' to 'thing I confess my job search to'?).

**The hard truth:** Your defining capability cannot be your first ship, and you didn't notice because your north star is a moat-and-retention argument wearing an adoption costume — it has no first session in it. The Intent Ledger's proudest claim ('it compounds, no cloud can reconstruct it') is the same sentence as its adoption death ('on install it is empty, and an empty memory is indistinguishable from a useless pet'). A vision with no answer for the first five minutes never earns a second session, and a spine no one is alive long enough to grow is just a beautiful corpse. Lead with the paw on the pixel; let the memory get built by the users the wedge brought you.
